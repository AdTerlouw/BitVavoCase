10:30 Pinar — Product Manager

Good instinct to go back to research first. That's the right call.

On "intent"—yes, it's basically what you said: what is the user trying to accomplish with this trade? But it's more nuanced than just "buy Bitcoin."

Let me break it down how I think about it:

Intent = the goal behind the action. Examples:

- "I want to buy some ETH and hold it for a while" → long-term investment
- "I think BTC is going to dip, I want to buy when it hits $58k" → opportunistic entry
- "The market's about to drop, I want to profit from that" → speculative short
- "I want to get out of this position right now" → exit/de-risk

The same user might have different intents on different days. And here's the thing—the right order type depends on the intent. A market order makes sense if you want in now. A limit order makes sense if you're waiting for a price. Margin/short makes sense if you're betting on a downward move.

Risk profile is different—it's more about the person than the trade:

- How much can they afford to lose?
- How experienced are they?
- How emotionally reactive are they to volatility?
- Do they understand leverage and liquidation?

So when the brief says "choose the right trading option based on intent and risk profile," it's really asking: can we help users match what they want to do (intent) with what they should be using (order type), while making sure they're not taking on more risk than they can handle (risk profile)?

That's the puzzle.
